What is AND Operator?


The "AND" operator is used to narrow down search results, requiring both conditions to be met. For example, in a search query, "dogs AND cats" would only return results that contain both "dogs" and "cats". In simpler term, AND Operator includes all words in an article.


History

The AND Boolean Operator traces its roots to ancient logic, where Aristotle described combining truths, but it was formally defined in the 19th century by George Boole in his algebra of logic.

Early computers implemented AND in hardware as logic gates, and by the 1950s it appeared in programming languages, with && for logical AND and & for bitwise AND in different languages . Today, AND Operator remains essential in mathematics, electronics, programming, databases, and search queries.
